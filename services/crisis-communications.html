Crisis Communications

Rapid-response messaging, stakeholder communication plans, and reputation triage when the story is moving faster than you are.

Twenty years covering breaking news means we've seen crisis response from the reporter's side of the phone call — the statements that made things worse, the silence that got filled in with speculation, and the responses that actually earned back trust. That vantage point is the core of how we handle crisis work: we know what a newsroom needs to stop guessing, and how fast they need it.

Crisis communications at C+M isn't just a plan that sits in a drawer. It's built to be usable in the first hour, when the instinct to say nothing is strongest and the cost of saying nothing is highest.

What This Typically Includes

  • Rapid-Response MessagingPre-drafted holding statements and key messages you can deploy immediately, adapted to the specific situation as it develops.
  • Stakeholder Communication PlansCoordinated messaging across employees, customers, investors, and press, so no audience hears conflicting versions of the story.
  • Media Q&A PreparationAnticipating the hard questions a reporter will ask and preparing leadership to answer them directly, without evasive language that erodes trust further.
  • Reputation Triage & MonitoringTracking how the story is spreading in real time across press and social channels to inform the next move.
  • Post-Crisis Recovery PlanningA longer-term plan for rebuilding trust and shifting the narrative once the immediate situation is stabilized.

  • Real-time reputation monitoring
